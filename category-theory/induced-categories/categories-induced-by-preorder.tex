    \pagestyle{plain} \currentdoc{note} \noindent
    If you have a \ex{Sets}{set} $P$ equipped with a \ex{Preorder}{preorder} $\le$, the \ex{Categories}{category} $P$ \textit{induced by the \ex{Preorder}{preorder}} is defined such that
    $$P(x,y) = \begin{cases}
        \{ x \to y \} & x \le y \\
        \emptyset & x \not{\hspace{-0.7ex}\le}\hspace{0.7ex} y \\
    \end{cases}. \hspace{3em} \forall x,y \in P$$

    \begin{proof}
        Let $\alpha: x \to y$, $\beta: y \to z$.
        Then $x \le y$ and $y \le z$, so by the \ex{TransitiveProperty}{transitivity} of \ex{Preorder}{preorder}, $x \le z$.
        Thus, $P(x,z) = \{ x \to z \}$, so $\beta\alpha : x \to z$ can be defined, and it must inherently be unique.

        Let $x \in P$. By the \ex{ReflexiveProperty}{reflexivity} of \ex{Preorder}{preorder}, $x \le x$, so $P(x,x) = \{ x \to x \}$, meaning there is a unique morphism $1_{x} : x \to x$.
    \end{proof}
